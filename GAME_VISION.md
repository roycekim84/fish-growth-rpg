# Fish Growth RPG — Game Vision (Reboot)

## Why
Prototype testing showed that the project feels closer to an io survival game than a true RPG. The project direction is therefore changing.

This is not because the prototype failed. It successfully identified what is fun and what is missing.

## New Vision
A small fish begins a great adventure.

The core fantasy is not becoming the biggest fish.
The core fantasy is exploring an ever expanding world of rivers, lakes and oceans while unlocking new species, abilities and stories.

## Core Gameplay Loop
Explore
→ Discover new regions
→ Meet new species
→ Complete quests
→ Unlock species
→ Gain new exploration abilities
→ Reach previously inaccessible areas
→ Continue the adventure

Hunting remains important, but it is a means of growth rather than the primary objective.

## Design Principles
- Exploration comes before grinding.
- Species represent horizontal progression.
- Levels represent vertical progression.
- Every new mechanic should encourage the player to visit a new place.
- Every biome should feel unique.
- NPCs, quests and bosses are first-class gameplay systems.

## Species System
Species are permanently unlocked.
Once unlocked, the player may switch to that species at safe evolution points.
Later progression may unlock limited field transformations.

## World Structure
The long-term world should expand from:
Pond → Stream → River → Waterfall → Lake → Estuary → Shallow Sea → Coral Reef → Open Ocean → Deep Sea → Special Biomes.

## Codex Guidance
Future implementation decisions should prioritize exploration, discovery and adventure over repetitive hunting loops.
When adding a new feature, first ask:
"Does this encourage the player to discover somewhere new?"
If not, reconsider the design.
