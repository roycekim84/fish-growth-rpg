# Fish Adventure RPG

> Small Fish. Big World.

## Document Status

- Version: 1.1
- Status: Active direction
- Role: Defines the long-term vision and evaluates major design decisions

## One-Sentence Vision

**Explore an ever-expanding world through the life and abilities of a fish.**

Fish Adventure RPG is a mobile exploration RPG in which the player begins as a small fish, discovers species and regions, gains new traversal abilities, and gradually reaches places that were previously inaccessible.

The goal is not simply to become the strongest fish. The goal is to discover more of the world.

## Core Fantasy

The player begins in a small and familiar habitat.

Beyond the visible boundary are waterfalls, caves, deep water, currents, ruins, hostile territories, and unknown species. By meeting creatures, completing quests, and unlocking new species, the player gains new ways to move through the world.

The experience should repeatedly create two questions:

> What is beyond that obstacle?

> Which species or ability will let me reach it?

## Core Experience

The intended emotional loop is:

```text
Curiosity
→ Exploration
→ Discovery
→ Growth
→ New ability
→ New region
→ More curiosity
```

Growth is not the final objective. Growth is a tool that enables further exploration.

## Design Pillars

### 1. Exploration First

Every major system should strengthen exploration. A newly discovered route, biome, creature, or secret should feel more valuable than a small numerical stat increase.

### 2. Discovery Over Grinding

Progress should come primarily from discovering places, meeting species, completing meaningful quests, and understanding the world—not from repeating the same action hundreds of times.

### 3. Every Species Matters

A species should provide a distinct way to play rather than serving only as a stronger stat package.

Examples:

- Salmon: climbs waterfalls or fights strong currents
- Octopus: enters narrow caves and interacts with mechanisms
- Flying fish: glides across gaps or above hazards
- Electric eel: powers devices and affects electrical obstacles
- Shark: specializes in direct combat and intimidation

### 4. A Living, Connected World

Regions, species, quests, traversal abilities, and environmental storytelling should connect to one another. New content should expand the world rather than feel like an isolated menu feature.

### 5. Respect Player Time

The game should minimize meaningless waiting, forced repetition, excessive farming, and unnecessary friction. Returning players should quickly understand what they can explore or accomplish next.

### 6. Optional Real-World Integration

GPS, photographs, and real-world exploration may provide optional collection, achievement, stamp, and title progression. These features should enrich the fantasy without blocking the main game or pressuring the player to reveal location data.

## Progression Philosophy

The primary progression loop is:

```text
Explore
→ Discover a region or species
→ Complete a quest or challenge
→ Unlock a species or ability
→ Reach a previously inaccessible area
→ Discover new content
```

Progression has two complementary dimensions:

- **Vertical progression:** levels, survivability, combat capability, and mastery
- **Horizontal progression:** species, traversal abilities, routes, interactions, and new play styles

Horizontal progression should remain essential throughout the game.

## Long-Term Player Goal

Over time, the player should be able to:

- Explore every major region and biome
- Discover and document a wide range of species
- Unlock species with meaningful gameplay differences
- Complete quests, achievements, stamps, and titles
- Defeat regional bosses and overcome traversal gates
- Build a personal record of both in-game and optional real-world exploration

## What This Game Is Not

Fish Adventure RPG is not intended to become:

- An idle or auto-hunting game
- A game centered on endless stat inflation
- A repetitive farming simulator
- A pay-to-win progression system
- A collection of disconnected features without world relevance
- A game in which real-world GPS participation is mandatory

## Success Criteria

The vision is succeeding when:

- Players frequently wonder what lies beyond a visible obstacle
- Unlocking a species changes what the player can do or where they can go
- Exploration feels rewarding even without combat rewards
- The world feels alive, interconnected, and worth revisiting
- Players want to complete their encyclopedia and exploration records
- Players think, “I’ll check just one more area before I stop.”

## Vision Guardrail

When a proposed feature conflicts with this document, the feature should be changed or rejected unless the project direction is deliberately revised and recorded in the decision log.
